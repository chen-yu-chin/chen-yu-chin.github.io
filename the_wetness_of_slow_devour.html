The 43.4-Channel sound work “The Wetness of Slow Devour" is developed during the residency at ZKM. The piece reinterprets underwater recordings from the Rhein Harbor and the surrounding rivers in Karlsruhe through listening, connecting, and misunderstanding.

The project explores the metaphorical and material connections between underwater monster mythology and the intensifying presence of underwater human activity in the Anthropocene. With the expansion of the shipping industry, military exercises, and global communication infrastructure, water bodies have transformed from a realm of myth and mystery into a site of complex ecological and technological entanglement.

Through the combination of electroacoustic composition and spoken word (poetry and speculative storytelling) “The Wetness of Slow Devour” explores the states and experiences of devouring and being devoured, narrating intersecting sonic discourses across technological, social, ecological, and aﬀective realities.

“The Wetness of Slow Devour” was created in the studios of the ZKM | Hertzlab as part of the artist-in-residency program "Spatial Storytelling" and was funded by the Fördergesellschaft ZKM / HfG e. V.. The recording of the piece was made by sound director Sebastian Schottke.
