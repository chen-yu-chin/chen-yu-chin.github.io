"On Sea Monster as Medium" is an ongoing artistic research project that proposes and connects multiple issues through the lens of sea monster studies. Working across artistic practice, speculative research, and writing to develop intersecting discourses on media, ecology, technology, colonialism, geo-relations, and identity.

The project currently includes "The Bloop Case Study" ( written and audio paper ) which examines how the formation of “Bloop” sea monster and its internet phenomenon link mythology stories, military agendas, undersea infrastructure, climate change, internet forums and media platforms into a complex cross-temporal ecosystem.

The radio play “The Wetness of Slow Devour" developed during the residency at ZKM, reinterprets underwater recordings captured around Karlsruhe, including the Alb River, Rhine River, and nearby harbor, through acts of listening, connecting, and misunderstanding. Combining electroacoustic composition, text, and vocal narratives, it creates a site of sea monster mythology and oceanic imagination to explore the experiences and sensations of devouring and being devoured.
